Rolling 20's Crip Gang

Category: Real-Life Gangs • Date: 10/12/2025

Overview

The Rolling 20’s Crips, also known as the West Adams Crips, are one of the oldest and most recognized Crip sets based in Los Angeles, California. Established in the 1970s, they have deep roots in the Crip movement and are known for their long-standing presence within South Central’s gang landscape.

Background

The Rolling 20’s originated around the West Adams area of Los Angeles. Their territory has historically been tied to nearby neighborhoods such as Jefferson Park and Mid-City. The gang became well-known through local conflicts and alliances, particularly their association with the larger Crip identity.

Over the decades, the Rolling 20’s have been linked to community events, internal disputes, and notable rivalries, including long-standing conflicts with various Blood sets.

Investigation / Details

Law enforcement and independent researchers have documented the gang’s activities for years, including drug distribution, weapon possession, and organized operations across Los Angeles County. In recent years, many members have been featured in federal indictments or task force reports targeting gang-related crime.

The Rolling 20’s Crips also maintain cultural influence — often referenced in West Coast hip-hop and street culture. Several artists and local figures have mentioned the set, further spreading its name into mainstream media.

  • Founded: Early 1970s, West Adams (Los Angeles)
  • Affiliation: Crip Alliance (Neighborhood Crip)
  • Primary Colors: Blue
  • Known Rivals: Blood sets in surrounding areas
